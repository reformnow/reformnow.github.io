There is a conversation that happens in nearly every Reformed church sooner or later. A pastor teaches that sinners are enslaved to sin and cannot choose God apart from sovereign grace. Immediately, someone objects: *"But I chose to come to church this morning! I choose things all the time. How can you say I have no free will?"*
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

在每一间归正教会，迟早都会发生这样一场对话。当牧师讲到罪人被罪奴役、若非主权恩典便无法选择上帝时，立刻就有人提出异议："可是我今天早上是自己选择来教会的！我每天都在做选择，你怎么能说我没有自由意志？"
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

This objection, however sincere, rests on a fundamental category error. The person in the pew has imported a concept of "free will" from modern secular psychology and applied it to a theological debate that was never about that concept at all. The result is that two entirely different conversations are being had in the same room, using the same words, and neither party realizes it.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

这个异议虽然真诚，却建立在一个根本性的概念混淆之上。会众将现代世俗心理学中的"自由意志"概念移植到了一场神学辩论中，而那场辩论从来就不是关于这个概念的。结果是，两场完全不同的对话在同一个房间里进行，用着同样的词语，双方却都未曾意识到。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

## 1. The Two Questions: They Are Not the Same
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

## 一、两个问题：它们根本不同
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

The confusion begins with the question each tradition is actually asking.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

混淆始于每个传统所真正追问的那个问题。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

**Historic Christian theology asks:** *Does a sinner, enslaved to sin since the Fall of Adam, possess the spiritual ability to repent, trust Christ, and please God—apart from a prior, supernatural work of grace?*
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

**大公教会正统神学所问的是：** *一个自亚当堕落以来就被罪所奴役的罪人，是否拥有在属灵上悔改、信靠基督、取悦上帝的能力——在无需先行超自然恩典介入的前提下？*
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

**Modern psychology asks:** *Are human decisions the product of a self-determining conscious mind, or are they ultimately caused by genes, brain chemistry, childhood trauma, and social environment?*
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

**现代心理学所问的是：** *人类的决定是自我决定的意识心智的产物，还是最终由基因、大脑化学物质、童年创伤和社会环境所决定的？*
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

Notice that these are questions in entirely different domains. The theological question is about the **human soul before a holy God**—it concerns sin, grace, and salvation. The psychological question is about the **human brain in a material world**—it concerns neurons, behavior, and causation. To answer the second question does not touch the first, and vice versa.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

请注意，这是完全不同领域中的两个问题。神学问题是关于**圣洁上帝面前的人类灵魂**——涉及罪、恩典与救恩。心理学问题是关于**物质世界中的人类大脑**——涉及神经元、行为与因果关系。回答第二个问题并不触及第一个，反之亦然。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

## 2. The Theological Debate: Luther vs. Erasmus (1525)
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

## 二、神学辩论：路德与伊拉斯谟（1525年）
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

The classic statement of the church-historical position came in 1525, when Martin Luther published *The Bondage of the Will* (*De Servo Arbitrio*) in direct response to Erasmus's defense of free will. It remains one of the most important theological documents in the history of the church.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

大公教会历史上这一立场的经典陈述出现于1525年，马丁·路德出版了《意志的束缚》（*De Servo Arbitrio*），直接回应了伊拉斯谟对自由意志的辩护。这至今仍是教会历史上最重要的神学文献之一。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

The debate was not about whether humans make real choices. Everyone agreed that they do. The debate was about whether the fallen human will retains any **spiritual power** to cooperate with grace unto salvation. Erasmus said yes—the will can, at some level, incline toward God. Luther said no—the will in the domain of salvation is entirely in bondage to sin, and only God's sovereign grace can liberate it.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

这场辩论并非关于人类是否做出真实的选择。双方都同意人会做选择。争论在于堕落的人的意志是否保留了任何**属灵能力**，能够与恩典合作而得救。伊拉斯谟的答案是肯定的——意志在某种程度上可以倾向于上帝。路德的答案是否定的——意志在救恩领域完全被罪所束缚，只有上帝的主权恩典才能将其释放。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

Luther was careful to distinguish two domains. In **civil or earthly matters**—choosing a spouse, managing a household, selecting a trade—the human will functions with genuine freedom. But in **spiritual matters**—turning to God, producing genuine repentance, generating saving faith—the will is bound. This is not a psychological claim about determinism. It is a theological claim about the devastation of sin.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

路德谨慎地区分了两个领域。在**世俗或民事事务**上——选择配偶、管理家庭、选择职业——人的意志拥有真实的自由。但在**属灵事务**上——转向上帝、产生真实的悔改、生发救恩的信心——意志是被束缚的。这并非一个关于决定论的心理学主张，而是一个关于罪之破坏力的神学主张。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

```quote
"Man's will is like a beast standing between two riders. If God rides, it wills and goes where God wills. If Satan rides, it wills and goes where Satan wills. Nor may it choose its rider... the riders themselves contend for the possession and control of it."
— Martin Luther, The Bondage of the Will (1525)
```
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

```quote
"人的意志如同站在两位骑手之间的一头牲口。若上帝骑它，它便照上帝的意愿行走。若撒旦骑它，它便照撒旦的意愿行走。它无法自选骑手……骑手们自己争夺着对它的占有与控制。"
—— 马丁·路德，《意志的束缚》（1525年）
```
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

This picture is not of a robot without genuine choices. It is of a creature with real desires and real choices—whose desires themselves are so thoroughly corrupted by sin that they will never, left to themselves, desire God. The problem is not the mechanism of the will but the **direction** of the will.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

这幅图景描绘的并非一个没有真实选择的机器人。而是一个拥有真实欲望和真实选择的受造物——其欲望本身已被罪如此彻底地败坏，以至于若任其自然，它永远不会渴望上帝。问题不在于意志的机制，而在于意志的**方向**。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

This is precisely the burden of Augustine before Luther, and Calvin after him. Writing against the monk Pelagius, who taught that humans can achieve righteousness through the exercise of their own natural will, Augustine established the orthodox position that has defined the church ever since:
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

这正是路德之前的奥古斯丁、以及路德之后的加尔文所承载的重担。奥古斯丁针对修士伯拉纠的驳斥——伯拉纠教导人可以凭借自然意志的运用来达到公义——奥古斯丁由此确立了至今仍定义着大公教会的正统立场：
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

```quote
"Without the grace of God, the will of man is not free, but enslaved, and that not by any external compulsion, but by its own inclination."
— Augustine of Hippo, On Grace and Free Will
```
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

```quote
"没有上帝的恩典，人的意志便不是自由的，而是被奴役的，这并非出于任何外部强制，而是出于其自身的倾向。"
—— 圣奥古斯丁，《论恩典与自由意志》
```
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

```bible
"Jesus answered them, 'Truly, truly, I say to you, everyone who commits sin is the slave of sin.'"
— John 8:34
```
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

```bible
"耶稣回答说：'我实实在在地告诉你们，所有犯罪的就是罪的奴仆。'"
—— 约翰福音 8:34
```
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

## 3. The Psychological Debate: From Freud to Libet
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

## 三、心理学辩论：从弗洛伊德到里贝特
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

Modern psychology entered the discussion of free will from an entirely different direction. The question is not about sin and God, but about whether the self is the true origin of its own choices—or whether something else (the unconscious, behavior conditioning, or neural firing patterns) is doing the "real" deciding.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

现代心理学从一个完全不同的方向进入了关于自由意志的讨论。问题不在于罪与上帝，而在于自我是否是其自身选择的真正起源——还是说，某种其他东西（潜意识、行为条件反射，或神经放电模式）才在做"真正的"决定。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

Sigmund Freud argued that the conscious mind is largely a facade. Our real motivations are buried in the unconscious—driven by repressed desires, childhood wounds, and primal instincts. We think we are choosing freely; in reality, we are acting out scripts written in our early years without our awareness. B.F. Skinner went further: the self is nothing more than a bundle of conditioned responses to environmental stimuli. There is no inner agent at all—only behavior shaped by reward and punishment.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

西格蒙德·弗洛伊德认为，意识心智在很大程度上只是一个表象。我们真正的动机埋藏在潜意识中——由被压抑的欲望、童年创伤和原始本能所驱动。我们以为自己在自由地选择；实际上，我们是在不知不觉中演绎着早年写就的剧本。B.F.斯金纳走得更远：自我不过是对环境刺激的条件反应的集合。根本不存在内在的行动主体——只有被奖惩塑造的行为。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

The most dramatic blow came in 1983 from neuroscientist Benjamin Libet. In his now-famous experiment, Libet asked subjects to flex their wrist whenever they chose, while monitoring their brain activity. The startling finding: the brain's "readiness potential"—a measurable electrical buildup indicating an impending movement—began approximately 550 milliseconds *before* the subject reported consciously deciding to move. The brain appeared to be initiating action before the conscious mind was aware of the decision.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

1983年，神经科学家本杰明·里贝特给出了最戏剧性的一击。在他那著名的实验中，里贝特要求受试者在任意时刻弯曲手腕，同时监测其大脑活动。令人震惊的发现是：大脑的"准备电位"——一种预示即将运动的可测量电信号积累——在受试者报告有意识地决定移动之前约550毫秒就已开始。大脑似乎在意识心智意识到决定之前，就已启动了行动。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

Materialist philosophers seized on this finding to argue that conscious free will is an illusion—a story the brain tells *after the fact* to give us the feeling of control we never actually had. On this view, humans are, in the words of the hard determinists, nothing more than "biological machines"—sophisticated carbon-based computers running programs written by evolution, genetics, and environment.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

唯物主义哲学家们抓住这一发现，论证说有意识的自由意志是一种幻觉——是大脑*事后*编造的故事，让我们感觉拥有从未真正拥有过的控制权。在这种观点下，人类用强硬决定论者的话来说，不过是"生物机器"——由进化、遗传和环境编程的复杂碳基计算机。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

## 4. The Same Word, Two Battlefields
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

## 四、同一个词，两个战场
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

We can now see the source of the confusion clearly. Both traditions use the phrase "free will," but they are fighting on entirely different battlefields:
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

我们现在可以清楚地看到混淆的根源。两种传统都使用"自由意志"这个短语，但它们是在完全不同的战场上作战：
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

| Dimension | Historic Church Theology | Modern Psychology |
|---|---|---|
| **Core Question** | Can a sinner choose God without grace? | Is the self the true origin of its own choices? |
| **The Enemy** | The bondage of sin | Biological and environmental determinism |
| **What "Free" Means** | Freedom *from sin* to obey God (John 8:36) | Freedom from external and biological compulsion |
| **Key Figures** | Augustine, Luther, Calvin | Freud, Skinner, Libet |
| **The Stakes** | Salvation and the glory of God | Moral responsibility and human dignity |
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

| 维度 | 大公教会正统神学 | 现代心理学 |
|---|---|---|
| **核心问题** | 罪人能否在无恩典的情况下选择上帝？ | 自我是否是其选择的真正起源？ |
| **所对抗的** | 罪的奴役 | 生物与环境的决定论 |
| **"自由"的含义** | 从罪中得自由，得以顺服上帝（约8:36）| 免于外部和生物强制的自由 |
| **代表人物** | 奥古斯丁、路德、加尔文 | 弗洛伊德、斯金纳、里贝特 |
| **所涉及的关键** | 救恩与上帝的荣耀 | 道德责任与人的尊严 |
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

When a modern believer hears "sinners have no free will," they hear the psychological claim: *"You are a biological machine with no genuine agency."* This is understandably alarming—it feels dehumanizing and seems to excuse all moral responsibility. But that is not what Luther or Augustine said. They said: *"You make real choices, but your choices consistently flow from a heart that is bent away from God, and no effort of that bent heart can unbend itself."*
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

当一位现代信徒听到"罪人没有自由意志"时，他们听到的是心理学主张："你是一台没有真正能动性的生物机器。"这令人警觉是可以理解的——它感觉是在贬低人的价值，并似乎为一切道德责任开脱。但这并非路德或奥古斯丁的意思。他们所说的是："你做出真实的选择，但你的选择始终源于一颗偏离上帝的心，而这颗偏离的心的任何努力都无法将自己扶正。"
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

## 5. The Deadly Errors on Both Sides
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

## 五、两种致命的错误
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

Confusing these two debates produces two opposite but equally serious errors.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

混淆这两场辩论会产生两种相反但同样严重的错误。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

### 1. The Error of Pelagianism: Treating Salvation as a Psychological Choice
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

### 1. 伯拉纠主义的错误：将救恩视为心理学意义上的选择
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

When Christians absorb the modern psychological notion of free will—that the self is self-determining and can direct itself toward any goal—they begin to unconsciously apply it to salvation. Faith becomes a simple act of willpower. The gospel becomes an offer one can accept or reject on neutral ground. Conversion becomes a matter of personal decision-making rather than supernatural rebirth. The initiative of grace is quietly replaced by the initiative of the human choice.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

当基督徒吸收了现代心理学关于自由意志的观念——即自我是自我决定的，可以将自己引向任何目标——他们便开始不知不觉地将其应用于救恩。信心变成了单纯的意志力行为。福音变成了一个可以在中立立场上接受或拒绝的提议。归信变成了个人决策的问题，而非超自然重生。恩典的主动性被人类选择的主动性悄然取代。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

This is precisely the Pelagian heresy—condemned by the church at the Council of Carthage (418 AD) and again at the Second Council of Orange (529 AD). It is not a minor theological nuance; it is a direct assault on the sovereignty of grace and the glory of God in salvation.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

这正是伯拉纠异端——它在迦太基会议（公元418年）和第二次奥朗日会议（公元529年）上被教会定罪。这不是一个细微的神学差异；它是对恩典主权和救恩中上帝荣耀的直接攻击。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

```bible
"For by grace you have been saved through faith; and that not of yourselves, it is the gift of God; not as a result of works, so that no one may boast."
— Ephesians 2:8-9
```
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

```bible
"你们得救是本乎恩，也因着信；这并不是出于自己，乃是　神所赐的；也不是出于行为，免得有人自夸。"
—— 以弗所书 2:8-9
```
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

### 2. The Error of Materialism: Dissolving Moral Responsibility
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

### 2. 唯物主义的错误：消解道德责任
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

On the other side, if we follow the hard-determinist reading of neuroscience to its logical conclusion, human beings become morally weightless. If a murderer's act was ultimately caused by his amygdala, his cortisol levels, and his abusive childhood, he cannot be held responsible any more than a rock can be blamed for falling. Courts have already seen defendants attempt to use brain scans as exculpatory evidence. Nihilism is the terminus of this road.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

另一方面，若将神经科学的强硬决定论观点推至其逻辑结论，人类便在道德上失去了重量。如果一个杀人犯的行为最终是由他的杏仁核、皮质醇水平和受虐的童年所导致的，那么他就无法被追究责任，正如一块石头不能为坠落而受责怪。法庭已经看到被告试图用脑部扫描作为无罪证据。虚无主义是这条路的终点。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

The historic Christian faith refuses both errors simultaneously. Against materialism, it insists that humans are **image-bearers of God** with genuine moral accountability—sin is genuinely *their* sin, chosen willingly, even if that will is in bondage. Against Pelagianism, it insists that **no sinner can save himself**, because the very heart from which choices flow is the problem. The solution is not a stronger exercise of the human will, but a divine act of regeneration that transforms the will itself.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

大公教会正统信仰同时拒绝这两种错误。针对唯物主义，它坚持人类是**上帝的形象**承载者，拥有真实的道德责任——罪真实地是*他们自己的*罪，是甘心乐意选择的，即便那意志是被束缚的。针对伯拉纠主义，它坚持**没有任何罪人能够自救**，因为选择从中流出的那颗心本身就是问题所在。解决之道不是更强烈地运用人的意志，而是一种神圣的重生之工，从根本上改变意志本身。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

## 6. The True Freedom the Gospel Offers
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

## 六、福音所赐予的真正自由
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

Here is the great irony: the doctrine of the bound will, properly understood, does not diminish human dignity—it is the very foundation for the most glorious kind of freedom.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

这里有一个巨大的反讽：正确理解被束缚的意志这一教义，非但不会贬低人的尊严——它恰恰是最荣耀的自由的基础。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

The psychological determinist says the will is bound to neurons and environment, and there is no escape. The Pelagian says the will is free enough to reach God on its own. But the historic Christian faith says something more radical than either: **the will was once beautifully free in Eden, was bound in the Fall, and is being liberated by grace**—not the pale "freedom" of self-determination, but the true freedom of doing what we were made for: loving and glorifying God without compulsion, without bondage, without resistance.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

心理学决定论者说意志被神经元和环境所束缚，无路可逃。伯拉纠主义者说意志足够自由，可以凭自己抵达上帝。但大公教会正统信仰所说的比两者都更为激进：**意志曾经在伊甸园中拥有美好的自由，在堕落中被束缚，并且正在被恩典所释放**——不是自我决定那种苍白的"自由"，而是做我们被造所为之事的真正自由：无需强迫、无需束缚、无需抵抗地爱戴和荣耀上帝。
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

```bible
"So if the Son makes you free, you will be free indeed."
— John 8:36
```
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

```bible
"所以，　神的儿子若叫你们自由，你们就真自由了。"
—— 约翰福音 8:36
```
{: .lang-zh lang="zh-CN"}

Modern psychology debates whether humans are puppets of **genes and environment**. Historic Christian theology debates whether humans are slaves of **sin and the devil**. Psychology tries to remove responsibility before the world; the gospel liberates the soul before God. These are not the same conversation. Until the church learns to tell them apart, she will keep answering the wrong question—and the people in her pews will keep misunderstanding the very grace that could set them free.
{: .lang-en lang="en"}

现代心理学争论人类是否是**基因与环境**的木偶；大公教会历史神学争论人类是否是**罪与魔鬼**的奴隶。心理学试图让人免除在世界面前的责任；福音却要在上帝面前释放人的灵魂。这两者根本不是同一场对话。除非教会学会区分它们，否则她将继续回答错误的问题——而她会众中的人也将继续误解那本可以释放他们的恩典。
